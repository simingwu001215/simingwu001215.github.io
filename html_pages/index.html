The Variation Between Clinicians and Hospitals

In the report, the analysis will focus on different treatment orders by examining the distribution of the number of patients seen and their corresponding percentages for each individual clinician. The dataset records child patients that are less than 12 months of age, presentng to ED with respiratory illness from 2015 to 2016.The objective is to investigate how variation is displayed at both the clinician and hospital levels. Users can interact with the scatter plots by zooming in and out. Additionally, if there is a specific clinician ID available, the "Search for Clinician" feature can be used to target that clinician's data. If the visualization layout appears too spread out, adjusting the zoom settings on the PC can provide a better view.

In the Chloral Hydrate visualisation, the violin plot reveals several insights:

  • Dandenong: No Chloral Hydrate use.
  • MMC-Clayton: Low usage despite more clinicians, all under 8%, indicating cautious use.
  • Casey: Few outliers in usage with fewer patients seen, but overall reasonable.

Summary: MMC and Dandenong are cautious, Casey shows reasonable use with minor outliers.

Overall, Ibuprofen is widely accessed compared to other medications across all hospitals. As the number of patients seen increases, the percentage of Ibuprofen usage decreases, but there is a lower limit. Notably, this reduction occurs in common intervals, such as

  • 8% to 10% for Casey and Dandenong
  • 40% across all hospitals

The violin plot reveals noticeable differences in the shape of Casey and Dandenong compared to MMC-Clayton when it comes to Ondansetron usage:

  • Casey and Dandenong: Both Casey and Dandenong exhibit more prominent outliers in their Ondansetron usage data. This contributes to a steeper shape in their respective violin plots.
  • MMC-Clayton: In contrast, MMC-Clayton appears to have a less steep shape and fewer outliers, suggesting that MMC-Clayton generally follows stricter guidelines or practices when it comes to Ondansetron usage.

    • In summary, the violin plot indicates that MMC-Clayton tends to have a more restrained and consistent approach to Ondansetron usage compared to Casey and Dandenong, which have more noticeable outliers and variation in their usage patterns, but still lie in reasonable range.

The violin plot shows that Paracetamol is commonly used in all hospitals, with consistent trends:

  • Paracetamol is widely accessed across all hospitals.
  • There is a noticeable reduction in usage percentages in a range roughly between 8% and 40% across all hospitals.

    • This suggests that there may be guidelines or practices in place across these hospitals that result in a consistent pattern of Paracetamol usage.

    In the usage of prednisolone, there are outliers among clinicians in all hospitals that exhibit a common characteristic: they are evenly spread out across a particular range of percentage values. This suggests that there may be specific scenarios or conditions where prednisolone is prescribed by clinicians in a consistent manner, leading to these evenly distributed outliers across hospitals.
